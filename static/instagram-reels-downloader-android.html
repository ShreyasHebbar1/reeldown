Method 2: Using Firefox or Other Android Browsers

VidsDownloader works with all major Android browsers including Firefox, Opera, Samsung Internet, and Brave. The process is identical — paste the link, wait for the preview, and tap download. Each browser saves files to the device's standard Downloads folder.

  • Firefox: Downloads go to Internal Storage → Downloads. Firefox also shows a download notification in the notification bar.
  • Samsung Internet: Files save to the Downloads folder and appear in Samsung's My Files app. A checkmark notification confirms completion.
  • Opera: Opera has a built-in download manager accessible from the sidebar — your Reel will appear there after downloading.

Where Are Downloaded Instagram Reels Saved on Android?

All downloaded files land in your Android device's Downloads folder in internal storage. You can access them through multiple apps:

  • Files by Google: Open the app → tap Downloads in the left menu. Your Reel will be listed there with the filename and file size.
  • Samsung My Files: Open My Files → Internal Storage → Downloads. The MP4 file will be visible.
  • Google Photos / Gallery: Open your gallery app and look for an album called Downloads. Your saved Reels will appear there automatically.
  • Chrome Downloads: Tap the ⋮ menu in Chrome → Downloads to see all recently downloaded files with quick access links.

Why did Chrome ask for storage permissions?

On older Android versions (Android 9 and below), Chrome requires explicit storage permission to save files to your device. Tap Allow when prompted. On Android 10 and later, this permission is handled automatically and you will not be asked.

Troubleshooting Android Download Issues

Download fails or gets stuck

Check your internet connection speed. Large video files (especially long Reels) may take longer on slow connections. Also ensure your device has sufficient free storage space — check Settings → Storage to confirm.

VidsDownloader not working inside WhatsApp or Telegram

In-app browsers embedded within WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram, or Facebook often block file downloads. Always open VidsDownloader directly in Google Chrome or another full browser. If you tapped a link inside an app, look for "Open in Chrome" or "Open in Browser" in the three-dot menu.

Link not recognized as an Instagram URL

Make sure you are copying the full Instagram URL. It should start with https://www.instagram.com/reel/ or https://www.instagram.com/p/. Short links from the app share sheet also work. If the link was copied from a notification or DM preview, go back to Instagram and copy it again using Share → Copy Link.
